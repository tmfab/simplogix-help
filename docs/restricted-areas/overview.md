# Restricted Areas

Restricted areas are physical zones on your site that carry hazards requiring controlled access — confined spaces, high-voltage areas, zones with chemical exposure, and so on.

## Why use restricted areas?

- Track active hazard zones across your site
- Link affected assets and defects to specific zones
- Make sure controls are verified on schedule
- Maintain a compliance trail of verifications and permits

## Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active** | The area is currently restricted and controls are in place |
| **Cleared** | The hazard has been resolved and the restriction lifted |

Active areas track how many days since their controls were last verified. The system flags areas as **overdue** if verification hasn't happened within 30 days.

## Viewing restricted areas

Three views are available:

| View | Description |
|---|---|
| **List** | Sortable data table with bulk actions |
| **Grid** | Card-based view with adjustable density |
| **Kanban** | Two columns (Active and Cleared) — drag cards between them to change status |

### Filtering

Filter by risk rating, status, plant area, verification date range, or created date range. Active filters show as dismissible chips.

### Analytics

Toggle the analytics panel from the toolbar to see:

- **Area Risk Heatmap** — plant areas vs. risk levels; click a cell to filter the list
- **Risk Distribution** — pie chart of Extreme, High, and Medium areas
- **Compliance Status** — donut chart showing what percentage of areas have up-to-date verifications
- **Verification Timeline** — monthly bar chart of verification events

### Bulk operations

Managers and Admins can select multiple areas to bulk verify, update status, export to CSV, or delete.
