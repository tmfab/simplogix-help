# Inspections

Inspections let you record the condition of assets at a point in time. Every inspection is tied to a specific asset, uses a checklist template, and creates a permanent record.

## Why inspections matter

Regular inspections help you:

- Catch issues before they become serious problems
- Maintain a compliance trail for auditing and reporting
- Track asset condition over time
- Identify defects that need corrective action

## Viewing inspections

The inspections page offers three views:

| View | Description |
|---|---|
| **List** | A sortable, configurable data table with filters and bulk actions |
| **Kanban** | A board with columns for Overdue, Due Soon, Scheduled, and Completed -- drag cards between Scheduled and Completed to update status |
| **Calendar** | A mini calendar with an upcoming inspections panel, plus a full month grid view. Click any day to see that day's inspections, or click **+** to quickly create one. |

## Filtering

Use the filters to narrow by:

- **Status** -- Scheduled, In Progress, Completed, Approved, Cancelled
- **Asset** or **plant area**
- **Date range**
- **Inspector**

## Scheduling inspections

From the calendar view, switch to **Schedules** mode to manage recurring inspection schedules. Schedules define:

- Which asset and checklist template to use
- Recurrence pattern (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly, or custom days)
- Auto-assignment to a specific operator (optional)
- Start and end dates
- Exceptions (skip or reschedule specific dates)
