# Inspections

Inspections let you record the condition of assets at a point in time. Every inspection is tied to a specific asset, uses a checklist template, and creates a permanent record.

## Why inspections matter

Regular inspections help you catch issues before they escalate, maintain a compliance trail for auditing, track asset condition over time, and identify defects that need corrective action.

## Viewing inspections

The inspections page has three views:

| View | Description |
|---|---|
| **List** | Sortable data table with filters and bulk actions |
| **Kanban** | Board with columns for Overdue, Due Soon, Scheduled, and Completed — drag cards between columns to update status |
| **Calendar** | Mini calendar with an upcoming inspections panel, plus a full month grid. Click any day to see that day's inspections, or click **+** to quickly create one. |

## Filtering

Narrow results by status (Scheduled, In Progress, Completed, Approved, Cancelled), asset, plant area, date range, or inspector.

## Scheduling inspections

From the calendar view, switch to **Schedules** mode to manage recurring schedules. Each schedule defines:

- Which asset and checklist template to use
- Recurrence pattern (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly, or custom days)
- Auto-assignment to a specific operator (optional)
- Start and end dates
- Exceptions (skip or reschedule specific dates)
